Permanence, Presence, Perfection

July 18, 2026

Your brain is a fascinating facilitator.

It can sort, process, and act within nanoseconds. 98% of the time, it is functioning on autopilot using pattern recognition from historical decision points. There is no deep analysis, nor a thesis needed to act.

There is a humorous yet intriguing word in Italian for this — cacofonico (cacophonous in English, albeit it is not in my daily lexical word bank...)

It is a word that sounds displeasing, or bluntly, "wrong."

Clacking, screeching, toe-tapping — all words that require you to stop, breathe, and ponder if that is the true pronunciation.

But that isn't the true focus of this post.

The true focal point is one hyper specific element of the processing lifecycle, the instant "grouping" component done subconsciously. Your brain automatically sorted the word into "displeasing," then the listener had to sort through the mess consciously.

The concept of "grouping" is primarily done with the numbers 1, 2, and 3 (see the above wordlist for reference).

There is a pleasing aspect to each of these figures, and they each have nicknames to accommodate.

One: Solo, Lone Wolf, individual, one-of-one, second-to-none

Two: Dynamic duo, pair, partners-in-crime, dyad

Three: Trio, Big Three, Troika, Threesome, trilogy

Humans naturally will sort ideas and objects into one of these three numerical buckets, or at a minimum, a multiple of them.

The advantage to this is it allows for an explanation into the who, what, why through subliminal context. Jordan, Pippen, and Rodman; Shaq and Kobe; Jeter and A-Rod; or Harry, Ron, and Hermione are all pop culture examples of how we view these "groups" within the subliminal context of the situation.

Groupings do not have to be people; they can also be ideas or abstract concepts.

As the title suggests, there is an inherent relationship between permanence, presence, and perfection.

Imagine the three words as a triangle connected. Better yet, see below:

Notice the general observations:

  1. The words flow when spoken
  2. The inherent and actual link between each
  3. The words' ability to interact with each other independently and within the larger grouping

To understand humans' longing for permanence in relationships, health, career, finances, happiness is to understand the concept of perfection or an ideal world.

Yet the omission of presence and "living in the moment" restricts comprehension of long-term permanence. Wouldn't you prefer to live now than hope for a future?

The inverse is also true. Presence in the moment yields "perfect" nights, summers, or moments but risks that moment being your last before dread, despair, and anxiety.

The mystical part revolves around perfection. Both presence and permanence could be perfect in their own way.

Comparing the two is frivolous as they are meant to be complementary (a dynamic duo, an iconic pairing, or power couple to continue our above theme).

Maybe that's the real takeaway.

Not which corner of the triangle to stand on, but the fact that all three are already yours — simultaneously, if you let them be.

Solo, duo, or trio. The number was never the point.

The relationship is.
