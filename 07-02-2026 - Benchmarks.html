Benchmarks

July 2, 2026

What if the benchmarks are wrong?

What if the standards we are setting for ourselves, technology, businesses, humanity, etc. are all incorrect and incomplete?

What percentage of our assumptions that we hold constant in our evaluation process can and should be challenged?

What are the implications for the past, today, and the future?

The evolution of how society sets goals and measures success has stagnated. It is all templated, duplicated, and repeated.

The numerical calculation has never been easier to predict, measure, flaunt, and hyper-focus on. Prices, profits, inflation can only go up, right?

But what about the miscalculation of the impact on humanity?

I would argue that the last 15 years have done irreversible damage to humanity’s outlook on business, politics, morality, ethics, technology, and relationships. AGI will exacerbate this trend at a pace we have never seen.

The adaptation of humans to social media, online banking, virtual school, driverless cars, mobile dating, and widescale smartphone adoption warrants praise. But the aftereffects are not lingering; they are howling. And the trend is just starting.

At what point do we wonder if we are measuring the wrong things? Fixating our future innovation on a flawed baseline?

What if there is no return, or worse, what would we even return to?
