Instant Gratification

Can humanity point to the precise moment when our expectations shifted from building to receiving?

I’m referring to the cornerstones of life I have listed in no particular order:

  • Business
  • Politics
  • Career Development
  • Religion
  • Family Structure
  • Sports

The list could go on infinitely, far beyond my limited coding skillset.

Take politics, for instance, through this sentence outline:

“I expected (insert politician / party) to have solved this problem by now! I voted for them so where is the action?!?”

Or in sports:

“This (insert owner / general manager) has had five years to win the championship! Enough is enough!”

Two competing ideologies emerge from these examples:

  1. Expectation of Reward
  2. Abbreviated Time Horizons

The simultaneous yet conflicting nature of gratification and time has produced a global society sprinting toward its own extinction.

Instant profits. Quick skillsets. Fast-tracked morality.

Each accelerant chips away at what’s left of ethical, long-term, morality-driven civilization.

Gone are the days of patient, scalable, sustainable growth, the kind that once yielded rewards beyond the dopamine rush of immediacy.

And there is no turning back.
